Overview
The University of Cape Coast Library system consists of the main library (Sam Jonah Library) with a seating capacity of 2,000, plus 31 satellite libraries located in various faculties, colleges, and departments.

2. Rules, Regulations & Conduct

Entry and Exit

Baggage: All bags and large handbags must be left in the Baggage Room (Ground Floor) before entering. Do not leave valuables (phones, money) in checked bags.

Inspections: You must show all books in your possession to security staff at the gate before exiting.

Identification: Insist on an identification tag for your bag when checking it in.

Prohibited Acts

No Smoking or Eating: Strictly prohibited inside the library.

No Phone Calls: Calls are not allowed in most parts of the library to maintain silence.

No Seat Reservation: You cannot "book" a seat by leaving items there for more than 10 minutes if you step away.

No Proxy Borrowing: You cannot borrow books on behalf of friends.

Disciplinary Actions & Penalties

Stealing: Classified as a grave offense; can lead to expulsion.

Defacing Books: Writing in, tearing, or damaging books will result in a surcharge.

Searches: The Librarian has the authority to search a student's room if theft is suspected.

3. Circulation Services (Borrowing Books)

Location: Circulation Desk (Ground Floor)

How to Borrow

Requirement: You must present a valid Student ID Card.

Duration: Books are issued for 2 weeks.

Renewal: You can renew a book for an additional 2 weeks if no one else has requested it.

Reservations: If a book is currently out, you can request to reserve it. It will be held for you for 24 hours upon return.

Fines & Lost Items

Overdue Fine: 50 Pesewas per book, per day.

Lost Books: You must report a misplaced book immediately. If not found, you must either:

Replace the book with a new copy, OR

Pay 3 times the current cost of the book.

Understanding Call Marks (What you CANNOT borrow)

Books on the Ground Floor, Africana, and Periodicals generally cannot be borrowed. Look at the spine label (Call Mark). If it has these prefixes, it is for Reference Only:

R: Reference

GH: Ghana Collection

DT: Africana/History specific codes

Borrowable books are primarily located on the 1st and 2nd Floors.

4. Departments & Physical Sections

Ground Floor

Serials & Media Unit: Houses current newspapers, magazines, and light reading materials for relaxation.

Reference & Reserve Collection: Dictionaries, encyclopedias, and high-demand books (Reference only).

Persons with Special Needs Section: Equipped with Braille resources, mobility aids, and computers for visually/physically challenged students. Services include transcribing exam scripts for lecturers.

Electronic Library (E-Library): Computer access, typing services, document exchange with other universities, and technical support.

First Floor

General Stacks: Main collection of borrowable books arranged by subject.

Second Floor

Africana Unit: Specialized resources on Africa and Ghana, including rare collections and theses. (Reference Only).

Law Library (East Wing): Dedicated collection for the Faculty of Law.

Development Information Unit: Supported by the World Bank. Houses publications on agriculture, environment, and global monetary reports.

Research Commons (West Wing):

Access: Restricted to Graduate Students and Faculty.

Facilities: High-end research environment and interdisciplinary workspaces.

Knowledge Commons (West Wing):

Access: Open to Undergraduate Students.

Facilities: Computer workstations and group study areas.

Discussion & Seminar Rooms: Located within the Commons. Must be booked in advance (Max slot: 4 hours).

Basement

Graduate Study Area: Quiet study cubicles restricted strictly to Graduate Students.

5. Digital Services (Website: crl.ucc.edu.gh)

To access these services, visit the website and navigate to the "E-Library" or "E-Resources" menu.

A. UCC Cat (Online Catalog)

Use: To find physical books inside the library or satellite libraries.

How to use:

Select UCC Cat from the menu.

Type keywords (e.g., "Food and Beverage").

Results show the Location (e.g., "Main Library" or "Hospitality Dept") and Call Number.

B. UCC Institutional Repository (UCC IR)

Use: Accessing Theses, Dissertations, and University Publications.

How to use:

Select UCC IR.

Browse by category (e.g., "Theses and Dissertations").

Click a title to view the abstract, then select "View/Open" to download the full PDF.

C. E-Resources (Journals & Databases)

Use: Accessing academic journals like JSTOR, Emerald, and EBSCO.

Off-Campus Access:

The databases recognize the University IP address automatically on campus.

From Home: You must select "Off-Campus Remote Access" and log in.

First Time Registration: Fill out the form (Name, Student Number, Institutional Email). Processing takes up to 2 working days.

D. Past Questions

Location: Under E-Resources > Past Questions.

Login: Requires your Student ID Number to authenticate.

E. Live Chat with a Librarian

Use: Real-time help for inquiries.

How to use:

Click the Chat icon or menu item.

Enter your Name, Email, and select a Department (e.g., General Enquiry).

If online, chat starts immediately. If offline, it sends an email.

F. Research Guides (LibGuides)

Use: Curated lists of resources for specific subjects (e.g., Biochemistry).

How to use:

Go to Research Guides.

Select your subject.

View a list of recommended books, journals, and databases specific to that major.
